Wiener Schnitzel

Although originating in Milan the schnitzel was quickly adopted by austrians and became, more or less, the stereotypical dish.

Ingredients

  • Bread crumbs
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • escalope of a veil
  • Flour

Steps

  1. Pound the meat until it is fairly thin, then put salt and pepper on it
  2. Mix the egg with a bit of milk in a bowl
  3. Cover the meat in flour, it should not have a thick cout but a slight flour cover over it's surface
  4. Dip it in the Egg-Milk mixture so it is drenched all over
  5. Then cover it with breadcrumbs, ensure it is properly covered with a thin layer but do not press it onto the crumbs
  6. Put it in a pan with a lot of oil, it has to float and bake it until it is gold brown
  7. Atlhough in austria often served with potatoes or rice it should be eaten with a light green salad
